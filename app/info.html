threebody

what is the three body problem?

Briefly: from some initial conditions, what are the motions of three point masses (bodies) under their mutual gravitational accelerations?

Less briefly: see this Scholarpedia article.

how does this page answer that?

The simulation page lets you set up a three body problem and then numerically integrate it in your browser. It uses the IAS15 integrator, a 15th order integrator that keeps the errors bouncing around at the level of machine precision. The integrator is available on github for more serious (as in non-browser based, scientific) useage.

how do I use this? what is it showing me?

First, you can choose from some preset initial conditions using the dropdown menu. This will set up some well known solutions, such as the figure-8. Once you choose one, click the play button to start integrating. The turtle and rabbit slow down and speed up the animation; the - and + zoom out and in.

The space plot

This shows the positions of the three bodies in Cartesian space, with little trails showing their recent positions.

The shape plot

This one shows the configuration of the three bodies, characterized by their size (moment of inertia) r:
and two coordinates x and y:

These points lie in a circle; the outer line of this plot corresponds to a colinear configuration. The center of the plot is when the bodies form and equilateral triangle; this is denoted by the three little colorful circles in a triangle. There are three points around the edge that correspond to collisions between two of the bodies; these are marked by the pairs of colored circles.

On the right is a gauge showing the size r of the system, though we plot (1 + r)/1. The top of the line (marked infinity) means the points are far apart, while the bottom of the gauge (0) shows a triple collision.

You can enter your own initial conditions or modify the presets; just type in values for the three bodies and click apply.
